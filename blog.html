The joy and efficiency of designing (first)

It's very tempting to throw oneself directly into constructing a game without considering much but a first idea. This may be just great if one wants to try out a game mechanic, and to see whether it's possible in code or in prototyping environments like Scratch and GameMaker.
But it's absolutely excellent to devote time for a decent graphic design, scaling elements, finding the right fonts and planning out the balance of the game in advance. Once a design has been carefully planned and thought through, it's so much easier and so much more fun to code and construct.
This was definitely the case with this website, which was laid out in Axure first, and coded later. For 2D games I recommend using Illustrator and Axure in conjunction, because you can easily drag elements in, scale them and preview what it looks like in a browser. Axure also provides options for some interactivity, so this calls for further exploration!

Dania Games Expo 2018

This year's expo at Dania Games was a blast! Ten games made by students were nominated for Game of the Year, and the winner was Bullseye - a PvP game made in the Javascript framework Phaser! The game is a two player PvP arena battle in 2D.
There were plenty of other impressive games, including Wartime, a VR experience in which you shoot planes from the side of a battleship. The game Ctuhlu in Denmark impressed several vistors with stunning lighting in well crafted 3D levels.
Other games included the side-scrolling mobile game Viking Vandals, a 3D platformer called Paper Hero, and many other impressive games made by students.
